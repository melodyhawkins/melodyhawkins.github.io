My Story

From Lawyer to Web Developer.

I grew up in Fresno and, like most of us, could not wait to leave. Eventually I graduated from UC Davis and after taking a year off, moved to Dallas, Texas to attend law school at Southern Methodist University. I absolutely loved it there. The people, the food, the shopping! But reality set in and I had to start practicing law. Fortunately, I passed both the Texas and California bar exams on the first try. (No joke, I was ready to fly back to Texas if I did not pass the California one). Anyway, I got a great job at a firm in Fresno and hit the ground running. I made partner after 5 Â½ years. Unfortunately, for me, I discovered that I did not enjoy being a lawyer. Even worse, I had no idea what else I wanted to be. So, I continued practicing law, even changing practice areas a few times, and firms a few times, including opening my own. Nothing changed, I simply did not enjoy being a lawyer. So, after some serious thought, and realization that my health needed to take precedence, I decided to take advantage of the blessings my hard work had given me, along with my extremely supportive family, and retire. After about a year, I realized I needed something to fill my days, since all of my friends were working, there was simply no one to play with. One night I was looking through Facebook and Geekwise Academy popped up, Websites for Beginners. I thought why not, it would be neat to learn how to make my own website. So, at around 1 in the morning, I signed up. It was one of the best decisions I have ever made. From the first day of class I have loved it and have never looked back. I look forward to helping you light up the world with your ideas through my skills as a web developer and designer.
